Data Analysis

Before reading this study

The analysis you are about to read is based on data produced by the US Census Bureau, a public and federal organisation. The data has been taken as it was produced by the bureau, meaning also with the terminology. The following analysis is only an exercice of data analysis based on those numbers. It does not aim to highlight real economical or sociological situations that would require much more data and further investigations if we wanted to produce a relevant analysis. In the same way, no political opinion is expressed by this analysis. The word race is only used beacause of the original data.

Please take notice that some minor changes have been made to the original data. Primarily we deleted some data that did not seem reliable or relevant. The data about the pacific community has been deleted because of a too much high margin of error risking to generate outliers. The data concerning "two or more races" has also been deleted beacause this notion seemed pretty unclear and could lead to misunderstandings. The consequence of these choices is that the row concerning Hawaii has been deleted because it no longer seemed relevant without the data about the pacific community. Concerning Puerto Rico, the row has been deleted because it was an outlier with very low median incomes that do not represent the situation on the continent.

Distribution of highest and lowest incomes by race

Looking at the pie chart on the left, we see clearly two races that dominate the others when it comes to be the race with the highest houseold's median income in the state. Those communities are the asian and white communities. The conclusion we can drow for now, based on this data, is that white and asian houseolds are generally richer than the others. It is also clear that asian houseolds are a step above the white ones. Indeed, the chart shows that in 3 US states out of 4 it is the asian houseolds that have the highest median income.

However this does not mean that they are always richer than other communities, huge differences can be hidden by this chart, and further analysis are needed to get a better insight of the economical situation of the communities in the US.

Looking at the pie chart on the right we can draw similar conclusions, as we did before. Three communities stand out, black, indian and other. Other is a bit problematic because we can not know for sure who it includes however based on what we know from the US population, it is highly possible that it consists of hispanic people.

Again the chart shows one commnity with a higher frequence of representation. In this case it is the black community. We can see here that black houseolds have the lowest median income in 1 out of 2 states. So we may say they ususally have less ressources than other communities, as well as for indians and other communities.

Does whites and asians really have higher incomes ?

The chart above clearly shows that yes, white and asian houselods are globally richer than other houseolds with a mean of median incomes accross the US around 62 000$ for the whites and 70 000$ for the asians. The main difference is in the repartition of the incomes. On the one hand, the asian repartition of incomes is very consistent. The data is equally reparted on the histogram and the very low skewness of -0.06 confirms it. However those statistics do not mean that asian houseolds are riche in every states, we can notice a standard deviation of 16 262$ with a coefficient of variation of 0.24 which pretty significant when it comes to the money a family has in a year.

On the other hand, the situation is slightly different for the white houseolds. The strong positiv skewness indicates that the data is unequally reparted left of the median. Most of the whites houseolds make a little bit less thant 58 564$ each year. Here the coefficient of variation is also significant and reminds us that even if white houseolds are globally richer in every state, differences can be found between the houseolds of different states. Does blacks, indians and others really have lower incomes ?

Based on the indicators that are the mean and the median we can state that accros the US states, black, indian and other houseolds are generally poorer. We use "generally" here because we have to remind us that the base studied data are alredy an indicator : the median income for each state.

The positive skwennes indicates that the incomes are generally lower than the median, so in some states those communities must be much richer. A significant coefficient of variation also needs to be taken into account, the mean incomes are also not representative, in fact in most states those communities are much poorer. The black community for exemple with a mean at 38 380$ has in fact many houseolds around 30 000$.
